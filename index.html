Virtue Score System

In 2060, the society uses a chip to record an individual's daily behavior, labor practice, language interaction and collaboration ability for life, which is a new social order that is based on genuine actions and social contributions, while also sparking intense debates about algorithmic power, the definition of fairness, and moral performance. The virtue score determines an individual's lifestyle, including education, medication, access to advanced technology, access to better households and applying for public sources. Citizens with scores from 7 to 10 are people who can get the first access to advanced technology, infrastructure, and convenient services. People who score 5–6 can get access to moderate sufficient public access and standard healthcare. People who score low from 1 to 4 only have access to outdated technology access, they have to wait and apply for advanced technologies and services. The system generates a Virtue Score from 1–10, determining access to housing, healthcare, and technology.

The chip implanted by medical institutions within 72 hours after birth, combined with passive NFC recognition, provides a system that generates the Virtue Score from 1–10, which is the core identity indicator for people.
